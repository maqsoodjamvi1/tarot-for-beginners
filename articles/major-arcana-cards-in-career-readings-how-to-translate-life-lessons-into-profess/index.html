When most people pull a Tarot card about their job, they look for a simple yes-or-no answer. Will I get the promotion? Should I quit this toxic boss? But when a Major Arcana card lands in a career spread, it completely changes the conversation.

Minor Arcana cards deal with the daily grind—office politics, client emails, balancing budgets, and small wins. Major Arcana cards, on the other hand, represent massive life themes. When one of these heavy-hitters shows up in a professional reading, it means your career isn’t just a way to pay rent right now. It is acting as a major classroom for your personal growth.

Here is how I translate those big, archetypal life lessons into practical, Monday-morning career steps.

Read the Room: Major Arcana vs. Daily Work

Let us say you are trying to decide whether to leave your job in accounting to start a freelance design business. You pull the Two of Pentacles, which clearly shows the juggling act of managing two different income streams. That is a tactical, day-to-day operational problem.

Now, imagine pulling The Fool instead. The Fool does not care about your Excel spreadsheet or your health insurance deductible. The Fool is asking if you are willing to step off a cliff and trust that your wings will grow on the way down.

Major Arcana cards force you to zoom out. They strip away the anxiety of your current quarter’s performance review and look at the trajectory of your entire professional life. If you treat a Major Arcana card like a Minor Arcana card—looking for a quick fix—you will miss the deeper shift that needs to happen.

Translate Archetypes Into Action Items

The trick to using the Major Arcana for career planning is translation. You have to take a philosophical concept and turn it into a concrete to-do list. Here is how I apply three common Major Arcana cards to real-world work scenarios:

  • The Magician: This card is about resourcefulness and skill mastery. If this appears when you feel stuck in a dead-end administrative role, it is a direct prompt to audit what tools are already on your desk. Stop waiting for your boss to hand you a training budget. Today, sign up for that free coding workshop, update your portfolio website, or schedule an informational interview with someone in a department you want to move into. You already have everything you need to start building your next chapter.
  • The Hanged Man: When this card pops up regarding a stagnant job search, it is deeply frustrating because it usually means pausing. But practically applied, The Hanged Man is an invitation to reframe your perspective. If applications aren’t landing, stop mass-submitting resumes. Use this forced standstill to completely redesign your resume layout, practice interviewing with a friend, or take a temporary contract role that gives you a totally different vantage point on your industry.
  • The Tower: Nobody wants to see The Tower in a career reading. It often accompanies sudden layoffs, company restructuring, or catastrophic project failures. Yet, practically speaking, The Tower destroys structures that were built on weak foundations. If your industry is shifting and your role is eliminated, use the shockwave to clear out old habits. Update your LinkedIn, reach out to former colleagues you lost touch with, and treat the sudden clearing as permission to pivot into a sector you were too comfortable to try before.

Run Your Own Career Spread

You do not need a complicated layout to get clarity on a professional dilemma. A simple three-card spread using the Major Arcana can map out a transition plan.

Pull one card for your current professional mindset, one card for the hidden obstacle you are ignoring, and one card for the ultimate outcome if you change nothing.

When you look at the cards, write down the literal physical actions they suggest. If your obstacle is The Moon, your action item is to stop guessing what your boss wants and ask for direct, written feedback. If your outcome is The Hermit, your action item is to stop networking at loud industry mixers and spend the next two weeks quietly building your independent portfolio behind the scenes.

Tarot does not predict a fixed professional future. Instead, it holds up a mirror to the choices you are already making, helping you spot the difference between a bad week at the office and a call for a total reinvention.

As you start applying these archetypal lessons to your own resume, job search, or daily workflow, pay attention to which cards keep showing up in your spreads. Your next professional move might become much clearer once you look at the bigger picture.
